2 Velocity

 

 

2.1.         Speed or velocity

Velocity is a vector quantity that characterizes the change in the movement of a given body relative to the reference body over time.

The term "speed" refers to a scalar value.

The designation is v, the units of measurement are m/s.

The average velocity is a vector quantity equal to the ratio of the entire movement to the time interval during which this movement occurred:

The average travel speed is a scalar value equal to the ratio of the entire path traversed by the body to the time interval during which this path is traversed:

Important!

To determine the average speed along the entire section of the path, it is necessary to divide the time into separate intervals and represent all the time as the sum of these intervals.

To determine the average speed for the entire time of movement, it is necessary to divide the path into separate sections and represent the entire path as the sum of these sections.

Instantaneous velocity is the velocity of a body at a given time or at a given point in the trajectory.

The instantaneous velocity is directed tangentially to the trajectory of motion.
