8 Strength of elasticity

 

Strength of elasticity

The elastic force is the force arising from the deformation of bodies, as a response to external influences. The elastic force is electromagnetic in nature.

Deformation - a change in the shape or volume of the body.

Deformation types:

Stretching is a type of deformation in which a load is applied longitudinally from the body, that is, coaxially or parallel to the points of attachment of the body.

Compression is a type of deformation, similar to stretching, with one difference in the way the load is applied, it is applied coaxially, but towards the body.

Bending is a type of deformation in which the straightness of the main axis of the body is violated.

 

Shear is a type of deformation in which a load is applied parallel to the base of the body. In the course of shear deformation, one plane of the body is displaced in space relative to the other.

 

Torsion is a type of deformation in which a torque is applied to a body caused by a pair of forces acting in the perpendicular plane of the body's axis.

 

Elastic deformations disappear after removing the load.

Plastic deformations remain after unloading.

Hooke's law: the modulus of the elastic force arising from the deformation of a body is proportional to its elongation

The elastic force is directed opposite to the movement of particles during deformation.

The support reaction force (N) is always perpendicular to the support.

The thread tension (T) is always directed along the suspension axis.
