Body weight

The weight of the body is the force with which the body, due to its attraction to the Earth, acts on the support or suspension (the force with which the body presses on the support or stretches the suspension).

Refers to the forces of an electromagnetic nature.

Measured with a dynamometer.

The unit of measurement is newton (N).

 

Weight of a body descending with acceleration or ascending with deceleration:

Body weight rising with acceleration or descending with deceleration:
