17 Power

 

Power - is a scalar physical quantity that characterizes the instantaneous rate of transfer of an amount of energy from one physical system to another during its use, and is generally defined as the ratio of transmitted energy to transmission time.

In the SI system, the unit of power measurement is the watt, which corresponds to 1 joule of energy transmitted within 1 second . Any numerical power value specified in information sources in common cases implies just such a second time interval.

According to the current international standards of the series, the power value is indicated by the symbol P in capital letters when writing formulas of mechanics and electrodynamics. Etymology of the designation-either from Lat. potestas, or from the English power.

EFFICIENCY – efficiency coefficient, equal to the ratio of useful work to spent, or useful power to spent:

Which work is useful and which is spent is determined from the condition of a specific task by logical reasoning. For example, if a crane performs work to lift the load to a certain height, then the work to lift the load will be useful (since it is for this purpose that the crane was created), and the work performed by the crane's electric motor will be spent.

So, the useful and spent power do not have a strict definition, and are found by logical reasoning. In each task, we must determine for ourselves what was the purpose of doing the work in this task (useful work or power), and what was the mechanism or method of doing all the work (spent power or work).
