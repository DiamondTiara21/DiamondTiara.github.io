Body impulse

The momentum of a body p (kg * m / s) is a vector physical quantity equal to the product of the body's mass by its velocity.

.

The direction of the impulse coincides with the direction of the velocity, since mass is a positive value:

.

The momentum is zero if the body is not moving (v = 0).

The total (total) impulse of a system of bodies is the vector sum of the impulses of all bodies:

The change in the momentum of the body is the vector difference between the final and initial momentum of the body:

.

Newton's second law:

The law of conservation of momentum: the total momentum of a closed system is conserved.

A system is called closed if the bodies included in it interact only with each other, and the influence of external forces can be neglected.
