It’s curiously... horsepower

 

why is engine power measured in horsepower

According to one version, Watt, trying to show the first buyer the benefits of using a steam engine, offered to conduct an experiment and compare the performance of his unit with a horse. However, the buyer decided to cheat and used his strongest horse in the experiment. But this did not help him, and Watt's steam engine showed much higher performance.

It was then that Watt coined the term horsepower and conducted several more tests. As a result of these experiments, he found out that in one minute a horse produces energy equal to 45 kilojoules. Thus, the term "Horsepower" appeared and from about the end of the 18th century, this unit of measurement became the main one for determining the power of steam engines first, and then internal combustion engines.
