Important!

Tangential acceleration characterizes the speed of change of the velocity modulus. Normal acceleration characterizes the speed of change in the direction of speed.

If aτ ≠ 0, an = 0, then the body moves in a straight line;

if aτ = 0, an = 0, v ≠ 0, then the body moves uniformly in a straight line;

if aτ = 0, an ≠ 0, the body moves uniformly along the curve;

if aτ = 0, an = const, then the body moves uniformly around the circle;

if aτ ≠ 0, an ≠ 0, then the body moves unevenly around the circle.

3.3.         Acceleration of gravity

Free fall is the movement of a body in an airless space under the influence of gravity alone.

All bodies in free fall, regardless of mass, fall with the same acceleration, called the acceleration of gravity.

The acceleration of gravity is always directed to the center of the Earth (vertically down).

The designation is g, the units of measurement are m/s2.

 

Important!

g = 9.8 m / s2, but when solving problems for simplicity of calculations, it is assumed that g = 10 m/ s2.
