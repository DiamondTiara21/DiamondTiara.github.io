Gravity

Gravity is the force with which the planet (Earth) pulls the surrounding bodies towards itself. Gravity is of a gravitational nature. The direction of gravity is vertical downward:

An artificial satellite of the planet (AES) is a body that revolves around the planet. The movement of artificial satellites occurs along ellipses, but for convenience, a simplified case is considered - movement in a circle. The linear speed of such movement is called the first cosmic speed.

 

M is the mass of the planet,

m is the mass of the satellite,

R is the radius of the planet,

H is the height of the satellite above the surface of the planet,

r is the distance from the center of the planet to the satellite (r = R + H is the radius of the orbit),

vI is the satellite's first space velocity.
