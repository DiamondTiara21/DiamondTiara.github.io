It’s curiously... formula for the rocket speed

 

The exact formula for the rocket speed was obtained in 1897 by K.E. Tsiolkovsky.

The first rocket design for space flights was proposed by Konstantin Eduardovich Tsiolkovsky, a Russian scientist, the founder of theoretical cosmonautics. He justified the use of rockets for space flights, concluded that it was necessary to use multi-stage rockets.

The ideas of Tsiolkovsky were brought to life by the Soviet scientist, design engineer S.P. Korolev. October 4, 1957 is considered the beginning of the space age. On this day, the design team led by Korolev launched the first artificial Earth satellite.

On April 12, 1961, for the first time in the world, a spacecraft carrying Soviet cosmonaut Yuri Alekseevich Gagarin was launched into Earth orbit. He opened the way to space. In space, it is impossible to use other engines than jet engines, since there is no support there, starting from which the spacecraft could get acceleration. Jet engines are used for airplanes and rockets that do not go beyond the atmosphere in order to maximize flight speed.

Jet engines are divided into two classes: rocket and air-jet. Air-jet engines are mainly used on airplanes. A modern space rocket is a very complex and heavy device consisting of a shell and fuel with an oxidizer.
