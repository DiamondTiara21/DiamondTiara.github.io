It’s curiously... Pierre Varignon

 

 Pierre Varignon is a French mathematician, member of the Paris Academy of Sciences, professor of mathematics at the Mazarin College. Varignon made his main contribution to statics and mechanics; in addition, his works are devoted to the analysis of hydromechanics. Varignon was the very first propagandist of differential calculus in France. In 1687, in his work "The Project of new Mechanics ..." Varignon gave an exact formulation of the law of the parallelogram of forces, developed the concept of the moment of forces. In the work "New Mechanics or Statics, the draft of which was given in 1687," Varignon gave a systematic exposition of the doctrine of the addition and decomposition of forces, of the moments of forces and of the rules for operating with them. The birth of modern kinematics can be considered the speech of Pierre Varignon before the French Academy of Sciences on January 20, 1700. Then, for the first time, the concepts of speed and acceleration were given in differential form, as mentioned earlier, P. Varignon was the first propagandist of this type of calculus. However, the content of the scientist's speech has not been fully established.
